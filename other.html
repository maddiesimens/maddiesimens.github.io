Streak

Streak is an app concept that I created as part of an interaction design course. Our prompt was to create a responsive web app that tracks something so I chose to create a simple tool that helps you track and build a new habit with a friend, an idea inspired by my friend Arjun.

You start by setting a goal that you’d like to complete each day—for example, walking the dog—and then invite a friend to join your streak by choosing their own goal. Each day, each person must log their goal in order to keep building their shared streak of days. This dual tracking motivates the user to maintain their momentum with the added accountability of not wanting to let down a friend by breaking their streak. Color is the app’s primary feedback system; one user is represented with blue while the other is yellow, and their shared progress is green.




Current

For my senior capstone project, I'm currently working with a team of three other students on a mobile app concept called Current. In a nutshell, Current is a personal contact management system (CMS). Current's goal is to make it frictionless to create new connections and effortless to maintain them. Our focus is replacing the act of exchanging business cards with a modern equivalent that leverages mobile technology. The app allows users to quickly exchange personal information cards with other users via QR code, easily add context like where/when you met, and create groups/tasks/reminders for follow up.

Beautiful Things

Below are a few posters that I designed for print-focused design courses. The first is both the simplest and my favorite. In typography class, we were asked to create a poster design that could be printed on a letterpress featuring one of our favorite quotes. We then had the opportunity to vote for one poster to print together at a local press. To my delight, my design was selected, and I got to hand print this poster at Union Press in Somerville, MA. As a lifelong letterpress nerd, it was a dream come true.



Donnie Darko

For this project, I was tasked with re-designing the promo poster for one of the university theater program’s past productions. I chose Donnie Darko because it’s also one of my favorite films. This ended up being an extra challenge because the feedback on my earlier versions was that they were too reminiscent of the film. I needed to come up with a visual metaphor that felt true to the plot without relying on the imagery from the movie. This split image photograph represents the parallel universes that drive the story.



Whitney

This piece is a classic typehouse poster for one of my favorite typefaces, Whitney by Tobias Frere-Jones. The uppercase ‘W’ takes center stage as one of Whitney’s most distinctive letterforms, and the dashed lines help to accentuate its humanist slants. The background contains a selection of other characters chosen for their special quirks that make Whitney unique.
